Question Link : https://leetcode.com/problems/longest-mountain-in-array/description/

*   Problem:
        Find longest subarray that forms a mountain:

            strictly increasing
            then strictly decreasing

*   Key difficulty:

        Need BOTH increasing and decreasing parts

*   Brute force:

        For each index:
            expand left and right

        Time Complexity:
            O(n^2) → too slow

*   Core idea:

        Precompute increasing and decreasing lengths

*   left[i]:

        Length of strictly increasing sequence ending at i

*   right[i]:

        Length of strictly decreasing sequence starting at i

*   Why this works:

        For each index i:
            we know how far it extends on both sides

*   Peak condition:

        left[i] > 1 AND right[i] > 1

*   Why?

        Must have at least:
            1 increasing step
            1 decreasing step

*   Mountain length:

        left[i] + right[i] - 1

*   Why subtract 1?

        Because peak is counted twice

*   Important invariant:

        left[i] captures uphill
        right[i] captures downhill

*   Time Complexity:

        O(n)

*   Space Complexity:

        O(n)
