Question Link : https://leetcode.com/problems/maximum-number-of-eaten-apples/description/

*   Problem:
        Each day apples grow.
        Each apple batch has expiry after certain days.
        Eat at most one apple per day.
        Maximize apples eaten.

*   Key observation:
        Apples have different expiry times.
        If we don't eat early expiring apples first,
        they may rot.

*   Core greedy rule:
        Always eat apple that expires earliest.

*   Data structure:
        Use MIN HEAP ordered by expiry day.

*   What heap stores:
        Indices of days when apples were grown.
        Comparator sorts by expiryDay = growDay + days[growDay].

*   Simulation:
        Iterate day by day.

*   For each day:
        1. Add new apple batch (if any).
        2. Remove expired batches.
        3. If heap not empty:
                eat one apple from earliest expiry batch.
                decrease its count.
                remove batch if count becomes zero.

*   Why remove expired before eating?
        Because expired apples cannot be eaten.

*   Important invariant:
        Heap always contains
        only valid, non-expired apple batches.

*   Loop continues until:
        No apples left
        and no more apples will grow.

*   Time Complexity:
        O(n log n)

*   Space Complexity:
        O(n)
