Question Link : https://leetcode.com/problems/maximum-subsequence-score/description/

*   Problem:
        Choose k indices such that:
            score = (sum of nums1 at chosen indices)
                    * (minimum nums2 at chosen indices)
        Maximize score.

*   Key observation:
        The minimum nums2 in chosen set
        determines the multiplier.

*   Important idea:
        If we fix a candidate as minimum nums2,
        we want the largest possible sum of nums1.

*   Strategy:
        Sort pairs by nums1 in descending order.

*   Why descending nums1?
        So we accumulate larger nums1 first,
        maximizing sum component.

*   Data structure:
        Use MIN HEAP ordered by nums2.

*   Heap meaning:
        Contains current selected elements.
        The smallest nums2 in heap
        is the multiplier.

*   Process:
        1. Add element.
        2. Maintain running sum of nums1.
        3. When heap size reaches k:
                compute score.
                remove element with smallest nums2.
                subtract its nums1 from sum.

*   Why remove smallest nums2?
        Because it is the weakest multiplier.
        Removing it allows exploring better combinations.

*   Important invariant:
        Heap contains at most k elements.
        num1Sum equals sum of heap elements.

*   Time Complexity:
        O(n log k)

*   Space Complexity:
        O(k)
