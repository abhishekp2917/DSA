Question Link : https://leetcode.com/problems/get-equal-substrings-within-budget/description/

*   We are given two strings s and t of equal length.
    Converting character s[i] to t[i] costs:
        |s[i] - t[i]|

*   We need the longest contiguous substring
    whose total conversion cost is <= maxCost.

*   Since substring must be contiguous,
    and we maintain a SUM constraint,
    Sliding Window is the perfect approach.

*   Maintain two pointers:

        left → start of window
        right → expanding end

*   As we expand right:

        Add cost of new index:
            cost += |s[right] - t[right]|

*   If cost exceeds maxCost:

        Shrink from left:
            subtract cost of s[left] -> t[left]
            move left forward

        Repeat until cost <= maxCost

*   At every step:
        Window [left ... right] is the longest valid
        substring ending at index right.

        Update maximum length.

*   Why this works:

        - Each index enters window once
        - Each index leaves window once
        - Total cost is maintained incrementally
        - Constraint is monotonic while shrinking

*   Time Complexity:
        O(n)

*   Space Complexity:
        O(1)
