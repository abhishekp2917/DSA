Question Link : https://leetcode.com/problems/split-two-strings-to-make-palindrome/description/

*   We are given two strings a and b of equal length.
    We can split both strings at the same index and form:

        prefix of a + suffix of b
    OR
        prefix of b + suffix of a

    We must check if any such combination forms a palindrome.

*   Brute force:
        Try all split points and construct strings → O(n²).

    Optimized solution:
        Two pointer greedy check → O(n).

*   Key observation:

        For prefix(a) + suffix(b) to be palindrome:

            a[left] must match b[right]

        We move inward while this condition holds.

*   Critical insight:

        Once mismatch occurs at (left, right),
        the remaining substring must be palindrome
        in either original string.

        So we check:
            isPalindrome(a, left, right)
            OR
            isPalindrome(b, left, right)

*   Why this works:

        Up to first mismatch,
        cross characters are already symmetric.

        After mismatch,
        we are forced to choose remaining part
        entirely from one string.

*   Why we check both directions:

        Because palindrome could form from:
            prefix(a) + suffix(b)
        OR
            prefix(b) + suffix(a)

        So we call checkCross twice.

*   Important invariant:

        While cross characters match,
        palindrome property is maintained.

        After first mismatch,
        only one continuous substring remains to verify.

*   Time Complexity:
        O(n)
        Each pointer moves at most n times.

*   Space Complexity:
        O(1)
