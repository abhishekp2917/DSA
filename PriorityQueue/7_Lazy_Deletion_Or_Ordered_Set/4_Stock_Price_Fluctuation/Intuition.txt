Question Link : https://leetcode.com/problems/stock-price-fluctuation/description/

*   Problem:
        Maintain stock price with:
            update(timestamp, price)
            current()
            maximum()
            minimum()

*   Key difficulty:
        Timestamps may be updated (corrected).
        So we must handle overwriting prices.

*   Core idea:
        Maintain two data structures.

*   Map 1:
        timestampPriceMap:
            timestamp → price

        Purpose:
            1. Retrieve current price.
            2. Detect previous price during correction.

*   Map 2:
        priceFreqMap (TreeMap):
            price → frequency

        Purpose:
            1. Maintain sorted prices.
            2. Quickly get:
                    maximum → lastKey()
                    minimum → firstKey()

*   Why frequency?
        Because multiple timestamps
        can share same price.

*   update(timestamp, price):
        1. Update currentTimestamp.
        2. If timestamp existed:
                reduce frequency of old price.
                remove if count becomes zero.
        3. Insert new price.
        4. Increase frequency.

*   current():
        Return price at largest timestamp.

*   maximum():
        Return largest price in TreeMap.

*   minimum():
        Return smallest price in TreeMap.

*   Important invariant:
        priceFreqMap always reflects
        prices of all active timestamps.

*   Time Complexity:
        update()  → O(log n)
        current() → O(1)
        maximum() → O(log n)
        minimum() → O(log n)

*   Space Complexity:
        O(n)
